# 🐕🔥 DOG FIRECRACKER SKIT — Visual Script

> **Concept:** Two dogs "team up" to light a firecracker, cover it with a bowl, and the explosion leaves one with the bowl on its head. No talking — pure visual comedy.
>
> **Duration:** ~10-15 seconds
>
> **Vibe:** Silly, mischievous, punchline-driven

---

## 🎬 PROPS NEEDED

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| 2x dogs | Your actors! Ideally one chill, one dramatic |
| 1x metal dog bowl | Stainless steel, reflective |
| 1x firecracker (red) | Small, safe, visual — or fake prop |
| 1x lighter | Standard BBQ lighter |
| 1x white bucket/bowl | For the "hat" payoff |
| Smoke effect | Optional: small smoke bomb or vape trick |

---

## 📋 SHOT-BY-SHOT BREAKDOWN

### SHOT 1 — "The Setup" (0:00–0:02)
**Camera:** Low angle, ground level, facing dogs
**Framing:** Both dogs standing side by side, metal bowl upside down on ground in front of them
**Action:**
- Dogs standing calmly behind the bowl
- Both looking at camera with innocent "we didn't do anything" faces
- Bowl is placed upside down on the ground (hiding the firecracker underneath)

**Tip:** Film this FIRST so dogs are calm. Use treats to keep them in position.

---

### SHOT 2 — "Lighting It" (0:02–0:04)
**Camera:** Close-up, ground level
**Framing:** Tight on dogs' paws + firecracker + lighter
**Action:**
- Dog 1 (chocolate lab): Paw resting on/holding the lighter (staged — handler positions paw)
- Dog 2 (brown mix): Holding bowl in mouth, watching intently
- Firecracker is visible, lighter is "clicking" near it
- Small flame/spark visible

**How to stage:** 
- Hold the lighter with your hand behind the dog, position their paw on it
- Film from angle that hides your hand
- Use treats to keep dogs still

---

### SHOT 3 — "The Cover-Up" (0:04–0:06)
**Camera:** Same low angle as Shot 1
**Framing:** Medium shot, both dogs + bowl
**Action:**
- Dog 2 drops/places the metal bowl over the firecracker
- Both dogs step back slightly
- Expectant looks — "what's gonna happen?"
- Start seeing smoke seep from under the bowl

**Tip:** This might take multiple takes. Reward dogs between attempts.

---

### SHOT 4 — "The Boom" (0:06–0:08)
**Camera:** Low angle, slightly wider
**Framing:** Both dogs + bowl + smoke
**Action:**
- Smoke billows from under the bowl
- Both dogs JUMP BACK / flinch dramatically
- Eyes wide, mouths open — classic "OH NO" reaction
- The firecracker is now burnt/charred on the ground

**How to get the reaction:**
- Use a loud clap or noise to startle them naturally
- Or use a real (small, safe) firecracker if legal/safe in your area
- Film their genuine surprise reaction

---

### SHOT 5 — "The Punchline" (0:08–0:10)
**Camera:** Same angle, hold steady
**Framing:** Both dogs in frame
**Action:**
- Dog 1 (chocolate lab): Has the WHITE BUCKET/BOWL on its head like a hat 🎩
- Dog 2: Standing next to them, looking at Dog 1
- Dog 1 sits there confused with bowl on head
- Dog 2 looks like they're laughing / judging

**How to stage:**
- Gently place the bucket on Dog 1's head (most dogs tolerate this for treats)
- Film from the side so you see both reactions
- The visual of the bucket-on-head IS the punchline

## 📱 FILMING TIPS

### Camera Setup
- **Phone:** Use portrait mode (9:16) for Reels/TikTok/Shorts
- **Height:** Get LOW — ground level makes dogs look bigger and more dramatic
- **Stable:** Use a phone tripod or prop against something steady
- **Lighting:** Natural daylight, golden hour (late afternoon) looks best

### Dog Wrangling
- Have LOTS of high-value treats ready (chicken, cheese)
- Film in short bursts — dogs lose interest fast
- Each "shot" is only 2-3 seconds of footage
- **Film the setup shots first** while dogs are calm
- Save the "reaction" shot for when they're most alert
- Have a helper position dogs while you film

### Editing
- **Cut fast** — this comedy works with quick cuts
- Add text overlays if needed (e.g., "Day 47: The plan is set")
- Slow-mo the explosion reaction for extra comedy
- End on the bucket-on-head shot — that's your punchline

## ⚠️ SAFETY NOTES

- **Firecrackers:** Check local laws. Use small, safe ones or fake props
- **Dogs:** Never force anything. If they're stressed, stop
- **Smoke:** Keep away from dogs' faces. Use small amounts
- **Bowl on head:** Only if your dog is comfortable. Remove immediately after filming
